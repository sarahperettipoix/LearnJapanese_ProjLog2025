Japanese



Japanese has three writing systems: Hiragana ひらがな, Katakana カタカナ and Kanji 漢字.

Hiragana and Katakana are two different ways of writing the same 46 sounds. Each character represents a sound such as "ki" or "ra". Hiragana is used to write native Japanese words, while Katakana is used for words borrowed from other languages.

For example:
Hiragana: Thank you - サンキュー
Katakana: Switzerland - スイス

Kanji is different than Hiragana and Katakana. This is because many of the Kanji characters are borrowed from the Chinese language and they do not always represent sounds you put together to form words. A single Kanji character can represent one word.

For example:
Kanji: Mountain - 山

Additionally, because of its complexity, Hiragana and Katakana are often used to tell you what a Kanji says and how to pronounce it.
